## What is a Prediction Market?

A prediction market lets people trade on the outcome of future events. Each outcome is represented by a token whose price reflects the market's collective belief in that outcome's probability.

- **Price = Probability** — If "Yes" trades at $0.65, the market thinks there's a 65% chance it happens.
- **Binary markets** — Two outcomes (Yes/No). Prices always sum to $1.00.
- **Multi-option markets** — Three or more outcomes. Prices still sum to $1.00.

## The PrometheX Stack

<Steps>
  <Step title="Market Creation">
    Admin creates a market via the dashboard. A `Prediction` contract is deployed on Arbitrum with the specified options, initial liquidity, and resolution parameters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trading (APMM)">
    Users buy and sell option tokens through the on-chain Automated Prediction Market Maker. The APMM algorithmically prices each option based on supply and demand. [Learn more →](/concepts/apmm)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolution">
    When the event occurs, the market owner asserts the correct outcome. The assertion goes through a challenge period (UMA Optimistic Oracle). If unchallenged, the market settles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settlement">
    Winners redeem their option tokens for base tokens (USDC) at a 1:1 ratio. Losing tokens become worthless.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Market Lifecycle

```
Created ──→ Running ──→ Settling ──→ Ended
                           │
                           └──→ Disputed ──→ Ended
```

| Status | What's happening | Who triggers it |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Created** | Market deployed, not yet open | Admin |
| **Running** | Trading open, APMM active | Automatic |
| **Settling** | Outcome asserted, challenge window open | Market owner |
| **Disputed** | Assertion challenged, awaiting arbitration | Any user |
| **Ended** | Settled — winners can claim | Oracle resolution |

## Token Model

### Current: ERC20 Option Tokens

Each market has separate ERC20 tokens for each outcome. When you buy "Yes", you receive Yes tokens. On settlement, winning tokens are redeemable 1:1 for USDC.

### Migrating to: Conditional Token Framework (CTF)

We're upgrading to the Gnosis Conditional Token Framework (ERC1155), the same standard used by Polymarket:

- **ERC1155** instead of separate ERC20s
- **Composable** with the CTF ecosystem
- **Split / Merge / Redeem** positions natively
- Standard tooling and indexer support

## Resolution & Disputes

PrometheX uses a **modular resolution system**:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="UMA Optimistic Oracle (default)">
    1. Market owner asserts the outcome and posts a bond
    2. **Challenge window** — anyone can dispute by posting a counter-bond
    3. No dispute → outcome accepted, market settles
    4. Disputed → escalates to UMA DVM (decentralized voting)
    5. Winner gets loser's bond (minus oracle fee)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Multisig Resolution">
    An N-of-M multisig of trusted resolvers votes on the outcome. Faster than oracle-based resolution, suitable for markets where speed matters.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Token Governance (planned)">
    Token holders vote on the outcome. Decentralized but slower. Best for high-stakes or controversial markets.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Gasless Experience

All transactions use **ERC-4337 Account Abstraction**:

1. User signs a `UserOperation` (not a raw transaction)
2. Platform's paymaster sponsors the gas
3. Bundler submits to Arbitrum's EntryPoint contract
4. Transaction executes — user pays nothing

Users never need ETH. They sign in with email and trade immediately.
