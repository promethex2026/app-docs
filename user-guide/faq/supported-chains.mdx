## Why Arbitrum?

You might wonder why PrometheX chose Arbitrum instead of Ethereum mainnet or another blockchain. Here are the reasons:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Low Fees" icon="coins">
    Transactions on Arbitrum cost a fraction of a cent compared to several dollars on Ethereum mainnet. This makes it practical for PrometheX to sponsor all gas fees on your behalf.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fast Transactions" icon="bolt">
    Trades confirm in seconds on Arbitrum, giving you a responsive, app-like experience. No waiting minutes for your trade to go through.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Ethereum Security" icon="shield-halved">
    Arbitrum is a **Layer 2 rollup** — it bundles transactions and posts them back to Ethereum for final security. You get Arbitrum's speed with Ethereum's safety.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Ecosystem" icon="globe">
    Arbitrum has a thriving ecosystem of DeFi protocols, bridges, and tools. This means more ways to get USDC, more integration possibilities, and a larger community.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What Is a Layer 2?

A **Layer 2** (or L2) is a network that sits on top of another blockchain (the "Layer 1") to make it faster and cheaper.

Think of it like an express lane on a highway:
- **Ethereum** (Layer 1) is the main highway — secure but crowded and slow during peak times
- **Arbitrum** (Layer 2) is an express lane — fast and cheap, but all the cars eventually check back into the main highway for security

Your funds on Arbitrum are ultimately secured by Ethereum — the most battle-tested blockchain in the world.

## Do I Need to Switch Networks?

**No.** PrometheX handles everything for you. When you sign in, the app automatically connects to the correct network. You don't need to:

- Add custom networks to a wallet
- Switch between chains manually
- Hold ETH for gas on any network

Everything happens behind the scenes, thanks to **account abstraction** (the technology that lets PrometheX manage blockchain interactions on your behalf).

## Will PrometheX Support Other Chains?

While Arbitrum is the primary focus, the PrometheX architecture is designed to be chain-agnostic. Future support for additional chains is possible based on community demand and ecosystem growth.
