## What Is Liquidity?

**Liquidity** is the pool of money that makes trading possible. When you buy or sell shares on PrometheX, you're trading against a pool — not against another person directly. That pool needs funds in it for trades to happen smoothly.

When you **provide liquidity**, you're adding your USDC to this pool. In return, you earn a share of the trading fees that other traders pay.

Think of it like owning a piece of a marketplace: every time someone buys or sells, you earn a small cut.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a market">
    Browse markets and find one you'd like to provide liquidity to. Active markets with lots of trading generate more fees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add liquidity">
    Enter the amount of USDC you want to add. The pool converts your USDC into a balanced set of shares across all outcomes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive LP tokens">
    You receive **LP tokens** (Liquidity Provider tokens) — these are like a receipt that represents your share of the pool. The more LP tokens you hold, the bigger your cut of the fees.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Earn fees">
    Every time someone trades in that market, a small fee (typically 1%) is collected. That fee is distributed to all liquidity providers proportionally. Your earnings accumulate automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove liquidity whenever you want">
    When you're ready, you can remove your liquidity. You'll receive your original USDC plus any fees you've earned, minus any **impermanent loss** (more on that below).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What You Earn

Your earnings come from **trading fees**. The default trading fee on PrometheX is **1%** of each trade, and all of it goes to liquidity providers.

**Example:**

- A market has \$10,000 in total liquidity
- You provided \$1,000 (10% of the pool)
- Someone makes a 500 trade → \$5 fee collected
- You earn 0.50 (10% of the \$5 fee)

<Tip>
  Markets with higher trading volume generate more fees. Look for popular, actively traded markets if you want to maximize your earnings.
</Tip>

## Understanding the Risks

Providing liquidity isn't risk-free. Here's what you should know:

### Impermanent Loss

**Impermanent loss** happens when the price of shares in the pool moves significantly from when you added liquidity. In prediction markets, this typically means:

- If one outcome becomes very likely (price moves toward \$1.00), the pool ends up holding mostly the losing side's shares
- When you remove liquidity, you might get back less USDC than you put in, because the pool's value has shifted

<Warning>
  The more dramatically a market's prices move after you add liquidity, the larger the potential impermanent loss. If you provide liquidity to a market where one side goes from 0.50 to 0.95, you may lose more in impermanent loss than you earned in fees.
</Warning>

### When to Provide Liquidity

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card icon="circle-check" title="Good Candidates">
    - Markets with high trading activity
    - Markets where prices are expected to stay relatively balanced
    - Markets with a long time before resolution
  </Card>
  <Card icon="triangle-exclamation" title="Be Cautious With">
    - Markets near resolution (prices moving sharply)
    - Low-activity markets (few fees to earn)
    - Markets where one side is already very high or very low
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Adding Liquidity — Step by Step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the market">
    Go to the market you want to provide liquidity to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Liquidity tab">
    On the market detail page, look for the **Liquidity** or **Pool** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your amount">
    Type in how much USDC you'd like to add. The interface will show you how many LP tokens you'll receive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Review the details and tap **Confirm**. Just like trading, there are no gas fees — PrometheX covers them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Removing Liquidity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the market">
    Open the market where you have an LP position.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Remove Liquidity">
    Choose how many LP tokens to redeem — all of them, or just a portion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your funds">
    You'll receive USDC back, including your share of accumulated trading fees. The amount may be more or less than what you put in, depending on impermanent loss.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  After a market resolves, any remaining liquidity is automatically converted. You can withdraw your share at any time after resolution.
</Info>
